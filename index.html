Benen Beelden

From 30 October to 31 December 2020 a series of ‘Benenbeelden’ are shown in the Central Hall of the WTC Amsterdam. This group of sculptures has been placed in the space in such a way as to create subtle lines of communication that you can transgress as visitor. When entering a casual waiting and working area one automatically relates to the other people in the space and positions oneself at the appropriate distance. After which most people engross themselves in their phone, laptop, tablet of magazine. The seven sculptures do the same.


The detailed legs clearly indicate that they are humanoid but the distorted or hidden body makes them mysterious as to who or what they are. They are open to interpretation: Is this our current society where everyone is hidden in their own bubble? Turning inward? Does the use of natural materials relate to the loss of nature around?


The world around us is in constant change which raises questions regarding our humanity and our position in the world. Change is also taking place in these sculptures with the growing of the plants used and the further drying of the other natural materials. Nothing is permanent, nothing remains as it is. This may be visible in the work.


Materials: black chamotte (clay) combined with live and dried natural materials.
